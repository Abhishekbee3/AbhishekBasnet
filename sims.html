FEA on Suspension and Steering Components

On the left is the FEA of a rotor hub at max load conditions to validate strength of bolt holes. In the middle is the FEA of the front suspension control arm simulating maximum force from shocks. On the right is the FEA of the rack and pinion mount simulating maximum shear force from bolts.


MATLAB Simulations

On the left is a spring mass damper displacement and velocity representation. In the middle is the theoretical output wave from a designed PID controller given a square wave input. On the right, a roughened paraboloid is plotted on a 3D graph. Vertical light rays along the z-axis are given as input and a reflection vector is simulated assuming the paraboloid has a reflective surface.

Suspension linkage lengths and joint locations determined from Lotus Software using kinematic analysis. Camber angle, toe angle, and bump steer studied with respect to suspension travel, chassis roll, and steering input.

Rear suspension on left. Front suspension on right. Entire suspension system shown in middle.
